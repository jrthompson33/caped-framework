remarks: "With LLMs, Jesse can generate complex visualizations like a parallel coordinates plot. Thus, it is tempting to assume that the AI assistant “removes” the chart authoring skill requirement en- tirely. However, Jesse’s success does not come from the AI agent; it relies on a combination of programming and visualization composi- tion knowledge. While the AI accelerated the generation of complex code, Jesse’s programming expertise was essential to first inspect and confirm code was syntactically correct but inappropriate in terms of chart design. Jesse then needed the conceptual knowledge to diagnose why the grouped bar chart failed and the precise visualization vocab- ulary to instruct the AI on the appropriate fix. Automated tools do not eliminate traditional skills; rather, they transform them. Chart authoring skills must evolve to encompass code verification, targeted prompting, structural critique, and the ability to steer AI agents away from conceptually flawed defaults and toward appropriate solutions."
